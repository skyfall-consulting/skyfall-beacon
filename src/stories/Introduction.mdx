# Skyfall Beacon

Beacon is the EdTech UI kit in the Skyfall family of design systems. It's a guiding light for modern learning products — a comprehensive set of React components purpose-built for course platforms, student dashboards, learning journeys, and educator tools.

Where Aegis (healthcare) is calm and clinical and Ledger (fintech) is sharp and exact, Beacon is **bright, structured, and motivating** — built to make progress feel visible and the next step feel obvious.

## What Beacon is for

Beacon accelerates product design and front-end engineering for learning experiences, including:

- **Student dashboards** — current courses, progress, recommended next lessons
- **Course and module players** — lesson lists, video/quiz states, completion flows
- **Assignment and assessment tools** — submissions, grading, feedback loops
- **Educator dashboards** — cohort analytics, learner performance, intervention surfaces
- **Onboarding and guided flows** — placement, goal setting, learning path setup
- **Achievement systems** — badges, milestones, streaks, certificates

## Who Beacon is for

Beacon is built for product engineers, designers, and teams shipping digital learning products that need to feel **encouraging, clear, and production-ready** from day one — without veering into either "kids app" cuteness or enterprise stiffness.

## Design philosophy

Beacon is organized around three ideas:

### Guided growth
Every surface should make the next step legible. Card CTAs, "next recommended lesson" states, milestone markers, stepper flows — Beacon prefers components that point forward.

### Visible progress
Learners should always know where they stand. Beacon ships with a strong progress vocabulary: progress bars, progress rings, completion states, streak indicators, milestone markers, and reward accents.

### Optimistic clarity
Bright surfaces, soft elevation, supportive empty states, and a luminous-sky palette. Encouraging without being childish; structured without being cold.

## System structure

Beacon is organized into two layers — a **shared core** (consistent across the Skyfall family) and a thin **EdTech extension** (Beacon-specific patterns).

### Components — shared core

The reusable foundation used across all Skyfall UI kits:

- **Foundations** — design tokens, theme, icons, typography
- **Inputs** — Button, TextField, Select, Checkbox, Radio, Switch, etc.
- **Data Display** — Table, DataGrid, List, DescriptionList, Avatar, Badge
- **Feedback** — Alert, Toast, Banner, Progress, Spinner, Skeleton
- **Surfaces** — Card, Paper, Modal, Drawer, Popover
- **Navigation** — Tabs, Stepper, Breadcrumbs, Pagination, SideNav, AppBar
- **Layout** — Stack, Grid, Container, Divider
- **Utils** — Portal, VisuallyHidden

### Beacon X — EdTech extensions

The thin specialization layer:

- **Charts** — Recharts-based KPIs, sparklines, line/bar/area for cohort analytics
- **Learning Patterns** — Course cards, lesson lists, progress rings, achievement badges, quiz result states, "next lesson" surfaces

## Design principles

1. **Guided growth** — Every surface points to the next meaningful step
2. **Visible progress** — Always make standing and trajectory legible
3. **Optimistic clarity** — Bright, supportive, never overwhelming
4. **Accessibility built in** — WCAG 2.2 AA, keyboard-navigable, screen-reader friendly
5. **Scalable to dashboards** — From single-lesson players to full educator analytics
